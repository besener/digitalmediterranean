Digital Mediterranean Project Guidelines:

This assignment consists of three (3) steps, each with its own deadline.

The three steps are: Propose, Prepare, and Finalize.

Propose (5%):

The first step is to submit a proposal for your Digital Mediterranean final project.

• In this course, you will explore the art and architecture of the medieval Mediterranean world, with a focus on key cities and sites. For your proposal, choose five locations and then select a monument or object for each. Using these selections, develop your final proposal in an academic paper no longer than two (2) pages.

• If you wish to change your selected locations or monuments as you progress, please consult with me for your intended revisions.

• Your proposal must adhere to academic standards, demonstrating clarity, coherence, and critical engagement with the material. Include a provisional title, clearly state the main thesis, and explain how the selected examples will support the argument.

• In your proposal, clearly state your main thesis and what you aim to demonstrate about artistic production and cultural interactions in the medieval Mediterranean world through your selected locations, monuments, or objects. Briefly describe how each selected example supports the overall argument, such as by illustrating connections, shared visual vocabulary, artistic exchange, or cultural interactions.

• Reflect on the broader implications of your project: What does your selection of sites and objects tell us about the medieval Mediterranean as a connected world? How do they demonstrate the movement of people, ideas, texts, and materials across political, religious, and linguistic boundaries?

• Format your paper according to academic standards: use left-alignment, double-spaced, 12-point Times New Roman font, and include page numbers. The paper must not exceed two pages in length.

• Please include your full name, your professor’s title and name, course code, and the submission date at the top left corner of the first page. Align this information to the left margin and use single spacing.

• The project proposal rubric can be found on page ten of this document.
