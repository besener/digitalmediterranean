Creating ArcGIS StoryMaps

September 1, 2025

Why Use StoryMaps in Academic Work?

ArcGIS StoryMaps is a web-based digital tool that enables users to integrate maps, narrative text, images, and multimedia into interactive stories. It is widely used in education, research, journalism, and public outreach to communicate spatial information in accessible and visually engaging formats. Although initially described as a tool for telling stories with maps, ArcGIS StoryMaps has evolved into a flexible platform that supports spatial analysis, research, and creative communication. Rather than solely describing a place, users can visually present locations, map connections, and encourage audience exploration. StoryMaps is utilized globally by educators, journalists, museums, and researchers to convey complex information in clear and innovative ways.

How Can We Use StoryMaps in Academic Work?

Using ArcGIS StoryMaps in coursework builds familiarity with digital tools, fosters critical thinking, and develops design skills. For example, students researching the medieval Mediterranean can present findings on art, architecture, and material culture in a visually appealing narrative. This approach encourages spatial thinking, digital literacy, and narrative design, and helps students share their work with a wider audience through digital platforms.

Incorporating ArcGIS StoryMaps into instruction strengthens subject mastery and practical skills. Students use StoryMaps to synthesize research on art, architecture, and material culture in compelling visual formats. This method fosters spatial awareness, digital fluency, and storytelling skills, enabling students to share their work with a wider audience.

Participation in this project encourages students to serve as investigators and storytellers. Students formulate research questions (a centaural argument) to demonstrate the movement of people, texts, ideas, and objects in the medieval Mediterranean world and design their StoryMaps to present their findings as a final class project. This integration of research and historical narrative skills supports their academic and professional development through the use of digital technologies and methods.

What is the Pedagogical Value of Using Digital Tools?

StoryMaps assignments prepare students to function as researchers and narrators, a combination valued in academic and professional development. For example, museums, cultural institutions, media outlets, and research organizations today use various digital platforms such as StoryMaps to engage with the public and share knowledge. Completing a StoryMap project provides valuable experience in digital scholarship and results in a polished, public-facing artifact suitable for portfolios, graduate school applications, or job searches.
