Final Project Paper

September 3, 2025

 

Digital Mediterranean Project Guideline:

This assignment consists of three (3) steps, each with its own deadline.

The three steps are: Propose, Prepare, and Finalize.

 

Prepare (10%):

The second step is to prepare and submit a paper for your Digital Mediterranean final project.

  • For each location, select a key visual that effectively represents the monument or object featured on your digital map.

  • Next, compose a visual analysis for each monument or object. As you have learned during the first and second assignments of this course, your visual analysis should identify, examine, and draw a conclusion regarding the significance of the monument or object at each location. Each analysis should not exceed 250 words.

  • Support your analysis with a minimum of two academic sources. Include at least two citations to appropriately credit the sources of information or interpretations. Refer to the Resources file on Canvas for guidance on the Chicago/Turabian citation style. Ensure all work is original to avoid plagiarism.

    • Consider the following themes or concepts when writing your analysis for each location:

  • Artistic exchange (how objects were similar and different in terms of form, style, iconography, and function),

  • Cultural and religious interactions (how communities interacted with each other in terms of artistic production),

  • Trade routes and networks (how people establish trade and commerce networks, where and how they traded and exchanged objects and ideas),

  • Migration and movement of people, texts, ideas, and objects (how people’s movement, including war, pilgrimage, trade, travel, and leisure, across the region shaped and articulated the art and architecture),

  • Architectural elements, plans, drawings, ornaments (what were some overlapping forms, styles, iconography images, how do you describe and interpret them?

Academic paper format:

  • Format your paper according to academic standards: use left-alignment, double-spaced, 12-point Times New Roman font, and include page numbers. The paper must not exceed two pages in length.

  • Please include your full name, your professor’s title and name, course code, and the submission date at the top left corner of the first page. Align this information to the left margin and use single spacing.

  • Submit your project paper as a Word document via Canvas. The text should not exceed 2,000 words, excluding works cited and citations. The paper must include an introduction with a thesis statement, visual analyses of selected monuments or objects, and a conclusion. Add a Works Cited page as a separate section at the end of your paper.

  • Do not include images or captions in the written paper. Include all images and captions in the ArcGIS StoryMaps.

 

Finalize (15%):

The third and final step is to finalize and publish your Digital Mediterranean final project on ArcGIS StoryMaps.

  • All content from the written paper submitted in step two must be incorporated into the ArcGIS StoryMap to complete and publish the Digital Mediterranean final project.

  • The ArcGIS StoryMaps submission must include the written paper, key visual images, citations, and a works cited section.

  • Adjust your share settings to “Public” and publish your ArcGIS StoryMap.
