Creating ArcGIS StoryMaps

September 1, 2025

Adjusting Your StoryMaps Settings:

How to Publish Your Story Map:

The final step to creating your story map is to publish it. This lets you set sharing permissions for your story map and any embedded content, such as a web map. If you edit your story map after publishing, click the Publish button again to apply the changes.

When you share your story map, the software will automatically ask if you want to change the sharing settings for anything included in your story map, like an ArcGIS Online web map. If you see this message, click Yes, Share these Items. If you do not change these settings, other people will not be able to see your content when they look at your story map.

  1. 1. Click the Publish button in the StoryMaps builder.

  2. 2. The default sharing setting is Private. This means that your story map is only visible to you.

  3. 3. You can update your sharing settings to share with a wider audience:

    • Organization: Your story map will be visible to anyone at Brandeis. Viewers will need to sign into their Brandeis ArcGIS Online account to view your story map.
    • Everyone (Public): Your story map is visible to everyone.
    • Group Sharing: Share content with members of an ArcGIS Online group. You can provide read-only access to your story map or editing access if you are a member of a shared update group. See Collaborate on a StoryMap for more information about using ArcGIS Online Groups.

     

    Update Story Card:

    The Publishing screen allows you to modify/change the story card for your story map, including the thumbnail image, title, and description. I suggest adding this information when you publish.

     

    Advanced Publishing Options:

    You can check or uncheck the allow duplication checkbox. Checking this box lets others duplicate your published story map. This permission allows users to save an editable copy of your original. Edits they make to their copy will not affect your original story map.

    You can also increase your story map's visibility by checking the show in web search results checkbox. You can uncheck it as well.
